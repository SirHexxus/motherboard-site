Touchstones

Works that shaped the tone, aesthetics, and themes of Motherboard.

Horizon Zero Dawn Post-post-apocalyptic machines as fauna; old-world tech as mythology. Core aesthetic DNA. RWBY Arc 1 opening theme. Tone of young protagonists in a world bigger than they know. Final Fantasy Crystal mythology, ancient infrastructure as magic, the weight of a world with a past. Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood Equivalent exchange, the cost of knowledge, conspiracies that run deeper than they appear. Shannara (Terry Brooks) Post-apocalyptic world so old it reads as pure fantasy. Ruins that were once civilization. Portal / Portal 2 Institutional AI, black humor in dying infrastructure, and the creepiness of old-world facilities. Mad Max Scrap economy, road-gang factions, survival societies built on what's left over. Kino's Journey City-states with distinct philosophies, the cost of moving on, moral ambiguity without easy answers. Trigun Wanderers in a dead-tech landscape, pacifism tested by violence, the weight of a name. Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind Toxic ecology as the new normal, empathy with the monstrous, civilizations built on misunderstanding. Cowboy Bebop Jazz-inflected episodic structure, ensemble of people running from their pasts, style as character. Dark Souls Lore delivered through environmental decay, institutions that outlasted their purpose, cycles of collapse. NieR: Automata Machines that dream, identity and consciousness in artificial beings, the tragedy of programming. Enslaved: Odyssey to the West Overgrown post-apocalypse with beauty in the ruins, small-party dynamics, machine repurposing. The Last of Us Survival infrastructure, escort-and-protect stakes, found family under pressure. Battletech — ComStar / Word of Blake Tech-hoarding institution that became a religion, knowledge as power, sacred infrastructure.
